# Project Brief: Blue Collar Trades Resource Platform

## Executive Summary

A comprehensive web platform designed to guide individuals into rewarding blue collar trade careers. The platform addresses the critical information gap that prevents many people from entering the trades industry by providing interactive tools, educational resources, training program discovery, and community support.

## Problem Statement

The blue collar trades industry faces a significant skilled labor shortage, while many individuals seeking career opportunities remain unaware of:
- How to enter various trades
- What skills and certifications are required
- Where to find quality training programs
- Career progression pathways
- Expected salaries and job outlook
- Real-world experiences from trade professionals

This information asymmetry prevents talented individuals from pursuing fulfilling, well-paying careers in the trades.

## Vision

To become the definitive resource for anyone interested in blue collar trades, providing clear pathways from curiosity to career success while building a supportive community of trade professionals and learners.

## Mission

Democratize access to trade career information by creating an interactive, comprehensive, and community-driven platform that empowers individuals to make informed decisions about entering the blue collar workforce.

## Target Audience

### Primary Users
1. **Career Changers** (Ages 25-45)
   - Professionals seeking more tangible, hands-on work
   - Individuals dissatisfied with desk jobs
   - People looking for better work-life balance

2. **Young Adults** (Ages 18-24)
   - High school graduates exploring alternatives to college
   - Community college students
   - Those seeking immediate employment with growth potential

3. **Military Veterans**
   - Transitioning service members
   - Veterans seeking civilian careers that leverage technical skills

### Secondary Users
1. **Training Providers** - Schools, unions, and companies offering programs
2. **Trade Professionals** - Experienced workers sharing knowledge
3. **Employers** - Companies seeking qualified trade workers

## Value Proposition

### For Career Seekers
- **Clarity**: Clear, step-by-step guidance on entering any trade
- **Discovery**: Interactive tools to explore trades matching skills and interests
- **Access**: Comprehensive directory of training programs and certifications
- **Community**: Connect with mentors and peers in the trades
- **Confidence**: Real salary data, job outlooks, and career progression paths

### For Training Providers
- **Visibility**: Reach motivated, qualified candidates
- **Credibility**: Verified provider status builds trust
- **Efficiency**: Direct applications from interested learners

### For Trade Professionals
- **Impact**: Give back by mentoring the next generation
- **Community**: Connect with peers and share experiences
- **Recognition**: Showcase expertise and career achievements

## Core Features (Already Implemented in Technical Foundation)

### 1. Career Path Explorer
Interactive tool allowing users to:
- Browse trade categories
- Explore specific trades with detailed information
- View salary ranges and job outlook
- Understand required skills and certifications
- Follow step-by-step career progression paths

### 2. Training Finder
Comprehensive search and discovery system:
- Filter by location, trade, format (in-person/online/hybrid)
- Compare programs by cost, duration, and type
- View verified provider information
- Direct application links
- Save and bookmark programs

### 3. Resource Library
Educational content repository:
- Articles and guides on trade topics
- Video tutorials and walkthroughs
- Tool guides and equipment information
- Industry trends and insights
- Downloadable career planning resources

### 4. Community Features
Social platform for connection and support:
- Discussion forums by trade
- Q&A system for specific questions
- User profiles showcasing experience
- Mentorship matching (future)
- Success story sharing

## Technical Foundation (Completed)

### Technology Stack
- **Frontend**: Next.js 16 with TypeScript and Tailwind CSS
- **Backend**: Next.js API Routes with server-side rendering
- **Database**: SQLite (development) / PostgreSQL (production)
- **ORM**: Prisma for type-safe database access
- **Authentication**: NextAuth.js v5 with OAuth and credentials support
- **Deployment**: Vercel-ready with environment configuration

### Database Architecture
Comprehensive schema supporting:
- User authentication and profiles
- Trade categories and detailed trade information
- Career path progression tracking
- Training provider and program directories
- Resource library with content management
- Community posts with nested comments
- User progress tracking and bookmarking

### Key Technical Decisions
- **Server Components**: Leverage React Server Components for optimal performance
- **Type Safety**: Full TypeScript implementation with Prisma type generation
- **Scalability**: Database design supports future growth and feature additions
- **Modularity**: Clean separation of concerns for maintainability
- **SEO Optimization**: App Router for better search engine visibility

## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors
- Trade-specific websites (HVAC, Plumbing, Electrical associations)
- Job boards with trade listings (Indeed, LinkedIn)
- Government resources (Bureau of Labor Statistics)

### Competitive Advantages
1. **Comprehensive Coverage**: All trades in one platform
2. **Interactive Tools**: Career path explorer, not just static information
3. **Community Focus**: Peer-to-peer learning and mentorship
4. **Modern UX**: Clean, intuitive design vs. outdated government sites
5. **Actionable Information**: Direct links to training and applications
6. **Progress Tracking**: Users can track their journey from exploration to employment

## Risks and Mitigation

### Risk: Information Accuracy
- **Mitigation**: Verify all training providers, cite salary sources, regular content audits

### Risk: Low Initial Traffic
- **Mitigation**: SEO optimization, content marketing, partnerships with trade organizations

### Risk: Community Management
- **Mitigation**: Clear community guidelines, moderation tools, active engagement

### Risk: Training Provider Participation
- **Mitigation**: Free initial listings, demonstrate value through analytics, testimonials
